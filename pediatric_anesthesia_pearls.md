**MALIGNANT HYPERTHERMIA 800-644-9737 www.MHAUS.org**
* **Signs:** tachycardia, arrhythmias, ↑↑EtCO2, ↑Temp, hypoxia, fasciculations, rigidity, ↑ K, myoglobinuria, acidosis
* **Treatment:** CALL FOR HELP, CALL HOTLINE
    1.  Hyperventilate with 100% FiO2, stop surgery and anesthesia
    2.  Dantrolene 2.5mg/kg as needed. 1mg/kg q6° when stable
    3.  Cool: Iced IVF, lavage stomach, bladder, rectum, ice packs (Goal <38°)
    4.  Labs: ABG, Lytes, Ionized calcium, glucose, CK, CPK, DIC profile
    5.  Treat acidosis with NaHCO3; treat ↑ K with glucose, insulin, CaCl
    6.  Keep UOP 2mL/kg/hr with hydration and diuretics, mannitol
    7.  Avoid Calcium Channel Blockers

### PAGE 2: IMG_0062 (輸液・輸血・カテーテル)

**FLUID AND TRANSFUSION PEARLS**
* Consider D2.5 in LR or NS (5 ml D50 + 95 ml LR/NS) as maintenance for children < 20 kg or those who have fasted >12 hours
* Use LR or NS for bolus fluids and insensible losses
* Use a Buretrol for children <25kg
* If on PAS/TPN, continue infusion intra-op, check glucose
* De-air all IV tubing. NO BUBBLES
* Use small bore (1.3 – 1.8 mL deadspace) tubing for infants

**Hourly maintenance:**
* 4 mL/kg for first 10kg
* 2 mL/kg for second 10kg
* 1 mL/kg thereafter
* *Shortcut: if wt >20kg then = 40 mL + wt*

**Fill volume chamber no more than 20 mL/kg**

**Allowable Blood Loss (ABL)**
Total Blood Volume (TBV) = weight (kg) X blood volume per kg

| Type | TBV |
| :--- | :--- |
| Preemie | 100 mL/kg |
| Term NB | 90 mL/kg |
| 6 mo | 80 mL/kg |
| 1 yo | 75 mL/kg |
| Teen | 65-70 mL/kg |

* **ABL Formula:** ABL = [(Hbg Pre) - (Hbg Post) / ((Hbg Pre + Hbg Post) / 2)] X TBV
* **Fluid Boluses** for hypovolemia: 10-20 mL/kg NS or LR
* **RBCs:** 10 mL/kg will raise Hbg by 1g/dL
* **Platelets:** 1 unit per 10 kg
* **2% & 3% Buffered Saline:** as 1/2 or 1/3 maintenance or 1-3 mL/kg bolus over 20 min, monitor serum sodium/hr. 2% infuse via PIV; 3% ideally via CVC
* **Albumin:** 5% -> 10 mL/kg; 25% -> 2-3 mL/kg
* **Hetastarch:** 10 mL/kg, no more than 35 mL/kg/day

**INTRAVASCULAR CATHETER SIZES**

**Arterial Catheter:** Sterile prep/drape. Consider using polyethylene 2.5F 2.5cm catheter. May use radial, DP, PT & femoral sites.

| Age | Arterial Catheter Size | Comments |

**Central Venous Catheter:** Consider ultrasound & an assistant. Sterile prep and full barrier precautions per Vascular Access Device policy. Post-insertion CXR required after placement. Use polyurethane catheters for venous insertion. Always check length to child prior to insertion.

| Weight (kg) | Catheter | Comments |

### PAGE 3: IMG_0063 (鎮静・区域麻酔)

**SEDATION / ADJUNCTS**

* **Clonidine:** PO 4-5 mcg/kg
* **Dexmedetomidine:** IV: 0.5-2 mcg/kg load over 10 minutes DO NOT PUSH. 0.2-1 mcg/kg/hr maintenance. Nasal: 1-3 mcg/kg
* **DiazePAM:** PO: 0.25-0.3 mg/kg (Do NOT give IM) IV: 0.05-0.1mg/kg
* **DiphenhydrAMINE (Benadryl):** PO/IM: 1-2 mg/kg (max 50 mg)
* **Ketamine:** *Always consider mixing with anti-sialagogue (atropine 0.02mg/kg)*
    * IM: 2-3 mg/kg sedation; 5-8mg/kg general anesthesia
    * *May add midazolam 0.1-0.15 mg/kg (stings with injection)*
* **Ketazolam:** PO: Ketamine 6 mg/kg, Midazolam 0.6 mg/kg, Atropine 0.02 mg/kg
* **Midazolam:**
    * Oral: 0.5-1 mg/kg max 20 mg, Can mix IV form with 10-15 mg/kg acetaminophen to make it more palatable
    * Rectal: 0.5-1m g/kg, Useful up to age 18 mos. (10 ml syringe attached to lubricated 14F suction catheter with connector removed)
    * Intranasal: 0.2-0.3 mg/kg (can be irritating)
    * IV: 0.05-0.1 mg/kg increments
* **Pentobarbital:** 2-6 mg/kg IM/PO (max 200mg); 1-3 mg/kg IV

**REGIONAL PEARLS**
* **General axioms:** *Always aspirate; use ultrasound when able; target nerve stimulator 0.4-0.5mA; know where intra-lipid is stored*
* **Single-shot-caudal:** good for procedures below umbilicus. 0.5-1.25 mL/kg
* **Epidural landmarks:** *Iliac crest line:* adult=L3-4, kids=L4-5, baby=L5-S1. *Spinal cord ends* L1 in adults vs L3 in infants <1 yo. *Dural sac ends* S2 in adults and S4 in infants. *Thoracic landmarks:* Scapular spine T3, inferior edge scapula is T7
* **Epidural test dose:** 0.1 mL/kg, Max 3 mL of local anesthetic with epi 1:200,000
    * ↑ HR (>10 BPM) or ↑ SBP (>15mmHg) in first minute
    * Change in T-wave amplitude of 25% (increase or decrease)
* **Infusion:** 0.2-0.4 mg/kg/hr (bupi); 0.8-1.6 mg/kg/hr (ropi); 1 cc/kg/hr (3%chloro)
* **Spinal:** positioning is key, usual max vol is 1-1.2mL; consider adjuncts
    * 0.5% bupi 0.1-0.2 mL/kg lasts 30-180 (80) minutes
    * 0.5% ropi 0.1-0.2 mL/kg lasts 25-240 (90) minutes
* **Penile Block:** NO EPI; insert 22 or 25g needle midline below symphysis pubis then 1 cm past penile fascia. Inject 2-3 mL midline, 2 o'clock and 10 o'clock.
* **Extremity Blocks:** LA dose typically 0.5-1 mL/kg. NEVER give a toxic dose especially when doing multiple blocks.

### PAGE 4: IMG_0064 (心内膜炎予防)

**INFECTIVE ENDOCARDITIS PROPHYLAXIS**
*Circulation 2007;116;1736-1754*

**Cardiac Conditions Associated With the Highest Risk of Adverse Outcome From Endocarditis for Which Prophylaxis Is Reasonable**
* Prosthetic cardiac valve or prosthetic material used for cardiac valve repair
* Previous infectious endocarditis
* Congenital heart disease (CHD)*:
    * Unrepaired cyanotic CHD, including palliative shunts and conduits
    * Completely repaired congenital heart defect with prosthetic material or device, whether placed by surgery or by catheter intervention, during the first 6 months after the procedure†
    * Repaired CHD with residual defects at the site or adjacent to the site of a prosthetic patch or prosthetic device (which inhibit endothelialization)
    * Cardiac transplantation recipients who develop cardiac valvulopathy

*\*Except for the conditions listed above, antibiotic prophylaxis is no longer recommended for any other form of CHD.*
*†Prophylaxis is reasonable because endothelialization of prosthetic material occurs within 6 months after the procedure.*

**Key SBE Prophylaxis Conclusions / Recommendations**
* Bacteremia resulting from daily activities is more likely to cause endocarditis than bacteremia associated with a dental procedure.
* Only an extremely small number of cases of endocarditis might be prevented by antibiotic prophylaxis, even if prophylaxis is 100% effective.
* Antibiotic prophylaxis is reasonable for all dental procedures that involve manipulation of gingival tissues, or periapical region of teeth, or perforation of oral mucosa, only for patients listed above.
* Antibiotic prophylaxis is reasonable for procedures involving incision or biopsy of respiratory tract or infected skin, skin structures, or musculoskeletal tissue only for patients with the underlying cardiac conditions listed above.
* Antibiotic prophylaxis solely to prevent infective endocarditis is not recommended for GU or GI tract procedures.
